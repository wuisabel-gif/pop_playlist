what is this

Pop, but maxed out.

Hyperpop takes everything pop is usually shy about — the sugar, the cheese, the volume — and slams the dial to 200%. Vocals get pitched into the clouds, basslines turn to glittering shrapnel, and the whole thing sounds like a candy store crashing through a server farm. It's internet-native, gloriously plastic, and proudly too much.

field guide

Speak the dialect.

Pitched-up vocals
Voices sped and lifted until they sound part-human, part-software. The signature texture.
Bubblegum bass
An early name for the sound: sugary melodies welded onto detuned, distorted low end.
The drop
The moment the candy coating cracks and everything blows out into glorious noise.
PC Music
The London collective whose hyper-glossy, ironic pop seeded much of the aesthetic.
Y2K revival
Chrome, butterflies, low-rise nostalgia for an internet that felt newer and shinier.
Digicore
The younger, rap-leaning, SoundCloud-born cousin sharing the same glitchy DNA.
Maximalism
The core philosophy: more layers, more sugar, more volume. Restraint is the enemy.
Glitchcore
Aesthetic of intentional digital breakage — corrupted, stuttering, beautifully broken.
