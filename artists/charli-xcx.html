Profile

Charlotte Aitchison started posting songs on Myspace as a teenager in Essex, releasing her debut album True Romance in 2013. Before she was a headline name she was a hitmaker for others — co-writing Icona Pop's “I Love It” and scoring her own breakout with “Boom Clap” and the Iggy Azalea collaboration “Fancy.”

Through the late 2010s she became the connective figure of a new avant-pop scene, working closely with the PC Music collective on the genre-warping mixtapes Pop 2 and albums like Charli and the rapidly self-produced lockdown record How I'm Feeling Now. Critics increasingly treated her as pop's R&D department.

Then came Brat (2024): a lime-green, club-leaning album that escaped the music press entirely and became a defining aesthetic of its year. She has since pivoted again — scoring the 2026 film Wuthering Heights and announcing the rock-and-electronic-leaning Music, Fashion, Film.
