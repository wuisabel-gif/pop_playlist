Profile

Zara Larsson won Sweden's Talang at ten years old, then spent her teens building a career that broke internationally with the ballad “Lush Life” and the MNEK collaboration “Never Forget You.” Her feature on Clean Bandit's “Symphony” became a UK number one and a global smash.

Across So Good and Poster Girl she established a polished, radio-ready Scandi-pop identity. With Venus (2024) she took ownership of her catalog by launching her own label, Sommer House, signalling a more deliberate, self-directed phase of her career.

2025's Midnight Sun — made closely with her partner and collaborator MNEK — drew on the feeling of an endless Swedish summer, blending dance-pop with drum-and-bass and earning some of her warmest reviews to date. A deluxe edition, Girls Trip, followed in 2026 with an all-women guest list.
