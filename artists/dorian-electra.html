03

Profile

Born in Houston to an artistic family, Dorian Electra began making music and videos around 2009 that fused pop with unlikely subject matter — philosophy, economic history and queer politics. An early viral video set the history of the gold standard to a synth-pop beat; the philosophy-club founder had found their form.

Their 2019 debut Flamboyant crystallised a sound that smashes EDM, nu-metal guitars and hyperpop into the same songs, paired with theatrical, gender-bending visuals that Electra directs themselves. My Agenda (2020) leaned into online subculture and irony, while Fanfare (2023) turned the lens on celebrity and the desire for fame.

A fixture of the experimental-pop world, Electra has collaborated across the scene — from Charli xcx and 100 gecs to Pussy Riot and the Village People — using maximalist songcraft to interrogate identity and performance.
