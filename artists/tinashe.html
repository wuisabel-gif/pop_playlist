Profile

Tinashe broke through in 2014 with her debut studio album Aquarius and its DJ Mustard-produced single “2 On” featuring ScHoolboy Q, a Top 25 Billboard Hot 100 hit that established her as a leading voice in the alternative R&B wave. After Nightride (2016) and the long-delayed Joyride (2018), she parted ways with RCA Records in early 2019.

Reinventing herself as an independent artist on her own Tinashe Music label, she self-released Songs for You (2019), 333 (2021) and BB/ANG3L (2023), earning critical acclaim for her creative control. Her 2024 single “Nasty” went viral and powered a full-fledged resurgence, leading into her seventh album Quantum Baby (2024).
