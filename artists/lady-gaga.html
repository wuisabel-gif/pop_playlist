Profile

Stefani Germanotta grew up in Manhattan, trained at the Tisch School of the Arts and played the downtown club circuit before exploding in 2008 with The Fame and its run of inescapable singles — “Just Dance,” “Poker Face,” “Bad Romance.” The theatrics were the point: she made pop spectacle feel like performance art.

Across the 2010s she refused to stand still, swinging from the anthemic Born This Way to the experimental Artpop, then to jazz standards with Tony Bennett and the rootsy Joanne. Her acting turn in A Star Is Born earned an Oscar nomination and a win for the song “Shallow.”

After the dance-floor catharsis of Chromatica, she returned to maximalist pop with 2025's Mayhem — and notched a global number one with the Bruno Mars duet “Die With a Smile.” Few artists have moved so freely between pop, theatre and film while keeping a devoted global fanbase.
