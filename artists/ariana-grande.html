Profile

Ariana Grande-Butera grew up in Florida and broke through on Nickelodeon's Victorious and Sam & Cat before launching a music career built around an extraordinary, whistle-register voice. Her 2013 debut Yours Truly announced a throwback-R&B sensibility that quickly scaled into arena pop.

The run from Dangerous Woman through Sweetener and Thank U, Next turned her into a defining star of the streaming era, with “7 rings,” “No Tears Left to Cry” and the record-breaking title track. She also became known for releasing music at a remarkable pace, sometimes only months apart.

After a quieter stretch, she returned with the introspective Eternal Sunshine (2024) and its 2025 deluxe, Brighter Days Ahead, while starring as Glinda in the blockbuster Wicked films — extending a career that has bridged TV, pop and Hollywood.
