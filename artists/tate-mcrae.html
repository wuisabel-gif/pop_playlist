Profile

Tate McRae began intensive dance training as a child in Calgary and reached a national audience as a finalist on So You Think You Can Dance. She posted original songs to YouTube on the side — and in 2020 the spare, aching “You Broke Me First” became a global hit, eventually certified multi-platinum.

Signed to RCA as a teenager, she expanded from confessional balladry into sharp, dance-driven pop across I Used to Think I Could Fly and Think Later, the latter spawning the smash “Greedy.” Her live shows lean heavily on her dance background, setting her apart from her pop peers.

2025's So Close to What became her first US number-one album, and her Morgan Wallen duet “What I Want” topped the Hot 100 — her first chart-topping single. Still in her early twenties, she's already two-time JUNO Artist of the Year.
