Profile

Amala Dlamini grew up in and around Los Angeles, studying piano and dance, and uploaded her first track to SoundCloud at 16. Signed to Kemosabe/RCA, she released the EP Purrr! and her 2018 debut album Amala — but it was the deliberately absurd viral single “Mooo!” that made her a household name.

Hot Pink (2019) delivered the chart-topping “Say So,” and Planet Her (2021) cemented her as one of pop's most versatile hitmakers, gliding between rap, R&B and dance-pop. She then swerved hard into harder hip-hop on Scarlet (2023), reshaping her image along with her sound.

With 2025's Vie — French for “life” — she pivoted again, into glossy, 1980s-indebted pop built around themes of love and romance, with a sole guest spot from SZA. Restless reinvention has become her signature.
